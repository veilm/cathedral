# Source: CATL sodium-ion battery expansion (ESS News, December 2025)

CATL announced at its December 28, 2025 supplier conference a significant
expansion of sodium-ion battery deployment for 2026, across energy storage
systems, battery-swapping infrastructure, passenger vehicles, and commercial
vehicles. CATL describes this as the beginning of a "dual-star" era where
sodium and lithium batteries develop in parallel.

Key specifications for the 2026 sodium-ion range:
- Energy density: up to 175 Wh/kg
- Operating temperature range: -40°C to 70°C
- Range capability: 500 km in passenger vehicle applications

Cost context: LFP battery pack prices for stationary storage fell to $70/kWh
in 2025, a 45% reduction from 2024. IRENA projects sodium-ion cell costs
could reach $40/kWh at scale. Average sodium-ion cell prices are around
$0.073/Wh ($73/kWh) as of late 2025, expected to fall to $0.035/Wh ($35/kWh)
by 2030.

In September 2025, Peak Energy deployed a 3.5 MWh sodium-ion battery energy
storage system at the Solar Technology Acceleration Center in Colorado —
the first grid-scale sodium-ion installation in the United States.
