# Source: BNEF solar LCOE 2025 anomaly & Form Energy scale-up (PV Tech, BNEF 2025; Utility Dive, March 2026)

Solar LCOE in 2025 saw an anomalous 6% rise. BNEF data shows fixed-tilt
solar PV LCOE hit $39/MWh in 2025, up from the prior year and above BNEF's
own projection of $35/MWh. BNEF solar analyst Jenny Chase attributed the
increase to: rising capital costs across energy markets, "power price
cannibalisation and curtailment" creating heightened project risks, and
elevated long-term interest rates in Europe (ten-year and twenty-year Euro
swap rates increased despite ECB rate cuts).

For context, solar LCOE has declined 97% between 2010 and 2025, the steepest
reduction of any tracked technology. Chase characterized the 2025 uptick as
temporary and "anomalous," not fundamentally altering solar's economics.

Meanwhile, battery energy storage systems (BESS) LCOE fell 27% year-over-year
to $78/MWh in 2025, diverging from solar and wind which both rose.

Other 2025 benchmarks: onshore wind $40/MWh, offshore wind $100/MWh, combined
cycle gas $102/MWh, conventional nuclear $258/MWh.

Form Energy is scaling iron-air batteries aggressively. A 300 MW / 30 GWh
deployment is planned as part of a Google-Xcel Energy agreement (announced
March 2026). Form's initial commercial projects include a pilot with Great
River Energy (Minnesota, summer 2025), two 10 MW projects for Xcel Energy,
and a 15 MW facility for Georgia Power (2026). Form targets $20/kWh at
manufacturing scale, with an 850,000 sq ft facility expanding toward
1 million sq ft by 2028. Market projections estimate 1-5 GWh total
iron-air deployments through 2027, scaling to 10-25 GWh/year in 2028-2030.
