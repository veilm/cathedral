# Source: Ore Energy iron-air pilot (ESS News, July 2025)

Ore Energy, a Dutch startup with around 40 employees and close to $23 million
in seed funding, subsidies, and grants, brought the first grid-connected
iron-air battery online in mid-May 2025 at The Green Village, Delft University
of Technology in the Netherlands. The pilot system is under 1 MWh capacity
with up to 100 hours storage duration. Ore Energy describes it as "the world's
first grid-ready iron-air system" and "the first long duration energy storage
system connected and installed within the European Union."

The technology uses iron, air, and water to store energy. Charging converts
iron oxides into metallic iron via electricity. Discharging reverses the
process — creating rust (iron oxide) and releasing energy. The system was
"fully-built" using European supply chain resources.

The pilot testing phase is planned for at least 6-12 months from May 2025,
evaluating cycle performance over multi-day charges, grid integration, and
environmental resilience. Ore Energy's planned commercial unit is a 4.2 MWh
containerized system.
